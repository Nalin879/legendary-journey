This is the first post on this blog, so let me explain what I'm trying to do here and what kind of writing you can expect.

## What this blog is about

At a high level, this is a notebook for the things I'm working on day to day as a software engineer. It's not a polished publication. It's more like a working journal where I capture the things I learn, the things I struggle with, and the things that change my mind.

The goal is simple: when I look back at this site a year from now, I want to be able to remember *why* I made the decisions I made, and reuse the patterns I figured out the hard way.

## What I'll write here

The writing will mostly be technical. Here's roughly what shows up:

- **Things I changed and why.** Not just "I switched from X to Y", but the actual reason. What broke, what got better, what surprised me.
- **Real problems I faced at work.** Bugs that took me two days to find. Architecture decisions that looked obvious in the design doc and felt terrible in production. Things I would do differently next time.
- **Small things that made a difference.** A better git alias. A useful pattern. A library I started using. The kind of content that doesn't deserve a full conference talk but is genuinely useful.

## How the posts are structured

Every post follows the same shape:

1. What the problem was.
2. What I tried first.
3. What actually worked.
4. What I would do differently.

If I can't fill out all four, it's probably not worth a post yet — it's probably just a thought. Thoughts go in a notes file, not here.

## Who this is for

This blog is for me first. But if you're a software engineer working on AI systems, or a developer trying to keep up with the pace of change, you might find some of it useful too. If you're a future me reading this six months from now, hello, you're welcome.

## What's next

The next post will probably be about how I set up this blog — what the stack looks like, why I chose it, and the small things I'd change if I were starting over. After that, I'll get into the actual technical content.

If you want to follow along, the easiest way is the [RSS feed](/legendary-journey/feed.xml) or to just check back here occasionally.
